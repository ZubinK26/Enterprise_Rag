# Information Security Policy

This policy defines baseline controls for workforce members, contractors, and enrolled devices that access company systems or process company information. Controls exist to mitigate credential compromise, phishing, malware, insecure collaboration habits, insider threats across corporate and hybrid remote environments wherever employees operate laptops or mobiles.

## Password requirements

Corporate passwords must remain confidential. Minimum length sits at fourteen characters. Password compositions must incorporate multiple character classes combining upper-case letters plus lower-case letters plus digits plus punctuation characters. Repeated characters beyond reasonable patterns are discouraged alongside dictionary words spelled forwards or mirrored keyboard walks. Workforce members must refrain from recycling identical secrets across personal recreational sites or unrelated vendors if those secrets also protect company identity providers.

Password rotation becomes mandatory after suspected compromise after security team communications after automated breach intelligence matches after forced resets from identity platforms. Storing secrets inside email bodies chat histories knowledge articles without encryption violates expectations. Prefer approved vaults ephemeral sharing links or cryptographic envelopes when rare emergency transfers arise.

## Multi-factor authentication (MFA)

MFA safeguards every interactive login capable of touching customer environments production consoles finance tooling email gateways source repositories administrative consoles contractor portals SaaS tenancy configuration surfaces. Workforce members must enroll hardware keys platform authenticators or approved soft tokens. Bypass requests require written manager endorsement plus quarterly security attestations outlining compensating detective controls auditing coverage session logging scope.

Individuals must remove factors when disposing hardware when suspecting coercion when experiencing repeated push fatigue attacks attackers exploit. Incident responders must revoke sessions revoke refresh tokens revoke device posture checks until identity re-proofing completes satisfactory.

## Device integrity

Corporate laptops mandate full-disk encryption mandated malware defenses mandated patch adherence mandated configuration profiles distributed through mobile device management tooling. Workforce members installing unsanctioned kernel extensions circumventing telemetry agents disabling disk encryption weakening screen lock durations triggers compliance violations remediated through revocation workflows.

Personally owned devices cannot access regulated customer datasets unless enrolled through limited bring-your-own-device pilots applying containerization remote wipe rights continuous compliance scanning.

Screens must lock automatically after five idle minutes unattended meeting room kiosks lock sooner preventing shoulder surfing exposures during visitor tours audits customer visits audits partner workshops.

Employees reporting stolen hardware must escalate immediately activating locate commands disk wipes forensic preservation steps alongside customer notifications when regulated data remnants possibly persisted locally.
